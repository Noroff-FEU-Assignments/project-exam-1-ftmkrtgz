The importance of reading books in child development

You don't have to wait for your children to grow up before reading story books to them. Research emphasizes the importance of parents reading books, especially before bedtime, starting from infancy. In this way, your baby is calmed by your voice and he/she learns sounds by the age of 1. At the same time, reading books, which is a very useful activity, goes well with your voice, which he loves and feels close to. Story reading before bed is an activity that supports the development of children. I recommend that you make reading a storybook while putting your baby to sleep a pre-sleep routine, such as taking a bath, starting from about the 6th month.

The contribution of reading storybooks to child development

The habit of reading storybooks acquired at an early age is very important for your child's language-cognitive development in later ages. In this way, your child learns more words, recognizes more sounds, objects and colors. When he turns 1 year old, he will recognize and know all the basic sounds he needs to speak. This is also important for speaking skills. When he gets a little older and begins to understand storybook content, his emotional and social intelligence improves. Now he can follow the events while reading a book to him, imagine, imitate sounds and characters, and act out. In addition, you can have a lot of fun together while reading books and contribute to your child's love of books. While reading a story book, the relationship you establish with both the characters in the book and each other will support your social development and ability to communicate correctly. Remember, your child takes you and your actions as an example and imitates them. When you read a book, you become a good example reader for him.

Personalized children's books

While reading storybooks is a very useful activity in itself, if these books are specially prepared for your child, much more enjoyable reading hours await you. Personalized books are special books prepared with your child's name or other characteristics in the book. If the story specifically involves your child, your child will become more curious while reading these books, will love the book and want to read it again and again.
